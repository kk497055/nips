Course Overview

Our Spoken English course is designed for students who want to communicate confidently in English for academic, professional, or personal purposes. Whether you're preparing for job interviews, want to improve your professional communication, or simply wish to speak English fluently, this course is for you.

Through interactive sessions, group discussions, and individual practice, you'll develop the skills and confidence to express yourself clearly in any situation. Our approach focuses on practical, everyday communication rather than just grammar rules.

What Makes Our Approach Different?

  • Practice-First Method: 70% speaking practice, 30% theory
  • Small Groups: Maximum 15 students for more speaking opportunities
  • Real Scenarios: Practice with job interviews, presentations, and daily conversations
  • Personalized Feedback: Individual corrections for pronunciation and grammar
  • Confidence Building: Supportive environment where mistakes are learning opportunities

Who Should Take This Course?

  • Students: Intermediate students preparing for higher education
  • Job Seekers: Professionals preparing for English interviews
  • Working Professionals: Those needing English for workplace communication
  • Business Owners: Entrepreneurs dealing with international clients

Week by Week

6-Week Learning Journey

Week 1

Building Foundation

  • Self-introduction and icebreaker activities
  • Basic pronunciation rules and common mistakes
  • Essential greetings and polite expressions
  • Practice: Daily conversation scenarios
Week 2

Pronunciation & Fluency

  • Word stress and sentence intonation
  • Linking words for smoother speech
  • Common pronunciation challenges for Urdu speakers
  • Practice: Read-aloud and shadowing exercises
Week 3

Everyday Conversations

  • Shopping, travel, and restaurant scenarios
  • Asking for and giving directions
  • Making requests and expressing opinions
  • Practice: Role-play activities
Week 4

Professional Communication

  • Business introductions and networking
  • Phone call etiquette
  • Email language for speaking contexts
  • Practice: Formal and informal scenarios
Week 5

Interview Preparation

  • Common interview questions and answers
  • STAR method for answering behavioral questions
  • Body language and confidence building
  • Practice: Mock interviews with feedback
Week 6

Presentations & Group Discussions

  • Structuring and delivering presentations
  • Participating in group discussions
  • Agreeing, disagreeing, and persuading
  • Final presentation and feedback session
